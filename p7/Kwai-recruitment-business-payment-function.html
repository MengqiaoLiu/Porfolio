Kuaishou recruitment business payment function

I worked as a UX designer for Kuaishou for two years, with one year focused on the recruitment feature in the Live Streaming department. My role involves collaborating with various team roles to clarify requirements, deliver user-centered experiences, and create clear prototypes.

Kuaishou, a leading short video platform and Douyin's(Tik Tok) top competitor in China, employs over 30,000 people and has 500 million monthly active users. The Live Streaming department's recruitment function facilitates communication between companies and job seekers.

The project aims to standardize company-influencer collaboration through a payment feature, helping companies recruit talent, increasing influencers' income, and aiding job seekers in finding jobs.

Problems

China's blue-collar job market is vast, with companies urgently needing to recruit large numbers of workers. However, blue-collar job seekers have limited access to information channels, making it difficult for them to find jobs outside of their personal networks. Kuaishou offers live-streaming recruitment sessions to help companies showcase their work environment and benefits. However, due to a lack of experience in live streaming, companies often fail to meet their recruitment needs. Some companies have started to hire professional influencers to conduct recruitment on their behalf, paying a commission fee. While this approach is effective, it raises concerns about transaction security.

Therefore, recruiters and influencers need Kuaishou as a third-party platform to help meet their needs and ensure secure transactions.

1. Job seekers often lack the skills to efficiently search for and filter information online, and learning new job search software presents a high barrier for them.

2. Companies using their own accounts for recruitment often have few initial followers, resulting in low traffic. Additionally, internal staff may lack the expertise to effectively promote through live streaming.

3. The lack of a third-party regulator in collaborations between companies and influencers leads to unsecured transactions and increases the risk of contract breaches, poor service, and disputes.

Solutions

We have taken this opportunity to create the Kuaishou Recruitment Platform Payment Feature: a platform that helps recruiters and influencers establish transaction rules and conduct secure transactions. This feature aims to help companies enhance their exposure and achieve recruitment goals, expand income channels for influencers, and make it easier for job seekers to find employment.

1. Simplifying the connection between job seekers and companies.
By introducing the concept of e-commerce into live-streaming recruitment, job positions are treated as products, and each resume submission is equivalent to a purchase order. When companies set up recruitment positions, they need to establish a price for each resume.

2. Standardizing the collaboration between companies and influencers, ensuring the security of commission payments and service quality. A wallet feature is implemented where recruiters must deposit funds to cover recruitment expenses. Once a recruitment position is set and published, the corresponding funds are frozen on the Kuaishou platform to ensure the recruiter has the necessary funds and will not default.
